
Integrated In Silico Characterization of the Bos taurus MSTN (GDF8) Gene

This project presents a comprehensive **in silico characterization** of the **Myostatin (MSTN/GDF8)** gene in *Bos taurus*, integrating sequence analysis, comparative genomics, protein structure prediction, and molecular docking to investigate its biological significance in livestock muscle development.

The study was carried out using publicly available bioinformatics databases and computational tools as part of a bioinformatics research project.

---

## Project Objectives

- Retrieve and curate nucleotide and protein sequences of the MSTN (GDF8) gene.
- Identify the coding sequence (CDS) and Open Reading Frame (ORF).
- Perform sequence similarity analysis using BLAST.
- Analyze exon-intron organization.
- Conduct Multiple Sequence Alignment (MSA).
- Construct a phylogenetic tree among livestock species.
- Identify conserved motifs and functional protein domains.
- Design gene-specific PCR primers and perform in silico validation.
- Predict the three-dimensional protein structure using AlphaFold.
- Identify potential ligand-binding pockets.
- Perform molecular docking with selected phytochemicals.
- Integrate structural, evolutionary, and docking analyses to evaluate the functional significance of MSTN.

## Molecular Docking Ligands

The following naturally occurring phytochemicals were investigated:

- Curcumin
- Quercetin
- Epicatechin

These compounds were selected because of their reported antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and muscle regulatory properties.


---

## Key Findings

- MSTN sequences are highly conserved among livestock species.
- Conserved protein domains and motifs support the functional importance of MSTN in regulating skeletal muscle development.
- AlphaFold generated a high-confidence three-dimensional model of the MSTN protein.
- PrankWeb identified potential ligand-binding pockets suitable for docking studies.
- Molecular docking demonstrated favorable interactions between MSTN and selected phytochemicals.

### Best Docking Scores

| Ligand | Best SwissParam Score (kcal/mol) |
|---------|----------------------------------:|
| Curcumin | **-7.8902** |
| Quercetin | **-7.4423** |
| Epicatechin | **-6.8891** |

Among the three phytochemicals, **Curcumin** exhibited the strongest predicted binding affinity toward MSTN.

## Biological Significance

Myostatin (MSTN/GDF8) is a negative regulator of skeletal muscle growth. Understanding its sequence conservation, structural characteristics, and interactions with bioactive compounds contributes to:

- Livestock genetic improvement
- Animal breeding strategies
- Functional genomics research
- Muscle growth regulation studies
- Computational drug discovery
